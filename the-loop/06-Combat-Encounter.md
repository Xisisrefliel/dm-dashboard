# The Loop — Combat Encounter

> The dragon fight, if they choose it. Optional — can be resolved without combat. But if your table wants to fight, this is how it works across loops.

---

## Principle: The Loop is the Prep

The party cannot win this fight on loop 1. They can barely survive it. The entire point of the time loop is that knowledge, positioning, and preparation carry across resets. By loop 5, they should have:

- The dragon's schedule
- Knowledge of its weak spot
- Dragon-piercing ammunition forged by Aldric
- Allied NPCs in position
- A tactical plan

This is earned. Let them have it.

## The Fight — Midday Ambush (Recommended)

The best window is midday, when the dragon drinks at the mill. It flies low (30 ft off the ground), it's briefly vulnerable, and the weak spot is visible.

**Setup (from previous loop's knowledge):**
- Party positions at the mill by 11:30
- Dalla coordinates guards on the north road to cut off retreat routes (they won't kill it, but they can prevent it from fleeing to the ridge)
- Aldric's dragon-piercing arrows distributed to the best archer
- Priestess Velda at the church, praying (gives +1 AC to all allies within 500 ft for 1 hour)

**The encounter:**
- Round 1: The dragon descends to drink. It notices the party. Surprise round if they set up with Stealth DC 15+.
- Round 2: The dragon reacts. If it took significant damage, it unleashes a breath weapon at the closest cluster. If it's smarter than angry, it speaks: "You break the pact with steel now? Bold."
- Round 3+: The fight continues. The dragon fights intelligently — it uses legendary actions, it targets healers, it tries to flee if below half HP. It doesn't fight to the death unless cornered.

**Weak spot mechanic:**
- Attacks targeting the gap under the left jaw: advantage if the attacker knows about it.
- A hit on the weak spot: extra 2d10 piercing damage + the dragon can't use legendary actions next turn.
- Calling out the weak spot to allies: requires an action or bonus action, but lets the whole party target it with advantage.

**Environmental advantages:**
- The mill: Full cover from the dragon's breath weapon if you're inside. The wheel provides partial cover from ranged attacks.
- The river: The dragon is wet when drinking. If a player can cast a lightning spell on the water under it, the dragon makes a DC 17 Con save or is stunned for 1 round.
- The treeline: The mill is surrounded by trees. The dragon can't fly effectively below 30 ft in the forest. If the party lures it into the trees, its fly speed is halved.

## The Fight — Sunset Confrontation (Harder)

If the party chooses to fight at sunset (18:00), the dragon arrives at full power, in the open, with no terrain advantages.

**Setup:**
- Party positions at the east gate or square
- All available NPCs assist (Dalla's guards, Aldric with a hammer, Velda's blessing)
- The village is in danger — collateral damage is real

**The encounter:**
- The dragon flies in at full speed. No surprise round.
- It opens with a breath weapon across the square. NPCs in the open die. This is intentional — it shows the stakes.
- It's harder here because: no terrain, no ambush, the dragon is angry and in "collection" mode, and there are civilians to protect.

**Winning this fight requires:**
- All the prep from the midday ambush
- Protecting civilians (Velda can shelter people in the church)
- A focused strategy on the weak spot
- Luck

## The Fight — If It Goes Wrong

The loop catches them. If the party dies, reset to dawn. They keep all knowledge. They've now seen the dragon's full combat pattern. They know what it casts, when it casts it, and how it targets. This is valuable.

**After a TPK:**
- The players roll new initiative at dawn. They describe where they're positioning based on last loop's experience.
- NPCs don't remember, but the players can give instructions faster: "Aldric, we need those arrows at midday. Dalla, guards on the north road."
- The second attempt is always better than the first. That's the point.

## The Dragon's Tactics

The dragon is intelligent and has fought adventurers before (the ranger 20 years ago left a scar, remember). It fights smart:

1. **Breath weapon first** — targets the largest cluster of enemies
2. **Targets healers** — it knows that the one in the back keeping everyone up is the biggest threat
3. **Uses legendary actions** — Wing Attack to knock prone, then Tail on the prone target
4. **Flee if below 25% HP** — it retreats to the ridge to lick its wounds. If the party doesn't pursue, the loop resets at sunset anyway (the village was saved, but the dragon will return)
5. **Negotiate if cornered** — if the fight is going badly for it and the players have the upper hand, it offers terms. "You have proven your strength. I will hear your counter-offer." This is the path to resolution 3 (renegotiation).

## Loot (If They Kill It)

The dragon's hoard is at the ridge cave. If they go there after killing it:
- Gold and gems worth ~2,000g
- A +1 shield with the old village crest (the original pact artifact)
- The ranger's remains (for burial)
- Scrolls and books from past tribute children's belongings — personal effects, journals, small treasures. This is gutting. It humanizes the "apprenticeships."
