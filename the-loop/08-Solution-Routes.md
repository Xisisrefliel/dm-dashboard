# The Loop — Solution Routes

> Step-by-step paths the players can take to reach each ending. They won't follow these exactly — players never do — but these are the "golden paths" that make each ending achievable.

---

## Route A: Diplomatic (Save the village without fighting the dragon)

**Core idea:** Find Lise, confront Ilsa, rally the village, face the truth together.

### Loop 1 — Die
- They wake up. They explore. They die. That's it.
- They learn: a dragon exists, it comes from the east at sunset, the village burns.
- Key detail to plant: if anyone notices Ilsa at the flower stall, describe her warmly. They should like her.

### Loop 2 — Warn
- They try to warn people. Nobody believes them.
- They learn: information alone isn't enough. They need proof.
- **Where to direct them:** The east gate. If they go east, they find the ridge (too far, they smell sulphur, turn back). The watchtower on the north hill (abandoned, they find the old lookout point but not the journal yet — they don't know to look for it).
- **What they might notice:** Ilsa staring east at 13:00 if they're near the east gate. The mayor ignoring her at 08:00 if they're in the square.

### Loop 3 — Investigate
- They're frustrated. They start probing.
- **Step 1:** Talk to Snik at the market. He mentions "big shape in the eastern hills" and "smell of sulphur." He also mentions the flower woman buying too much food. This is the thread for Ilsa.
- **Step 2:** Go east to the watchtower on the north hill. They can find this now because they're looking for information. Inside: the ranger's journal describing the dragon's patterns, the weak spot, and the last entry: "It came for the tribute. I tried to stop it."
- **Step 3:** Visit the mayor's open audience at 10:00. If they ask about the east, he deflects. If they ask about Ilsa, he tenses visibly and says "she's handled."
- **Step 4:** Follow Ilsa at 13:00 to the east gate. She's staring east. If approached, she says "the view is nice this time of day." It's not.
- **Step 5:** Follow Ilsa at 16:00 to the graveyard. She stands at her husband's grave. If they talk to her, she's calmer here: "He understood about apprenticing. He would have given her up. I can't." She clams up after "her." The players now know Ilsa has a daughter who should have been apprenticed.

### Loop 4 — Reveal
- They know Ilsa is hiding something. They know the village sends children east. Time to dig.
- **Step 1:** Break into the mayor's office while he's in closed council (14:00-15:00). DC 15 to pick the lock, or DC 20 if he's inside. Find the letters mentioning Ilsa, "the arrangement," and "the tribute."
- **Step 2:** Confront Ilsa with the letters. She breaks: "Yes, Lise is alive. She's here. I hid her." And then: "I've had this conversation before. I've had it so many times." The players now know: Ilsa's grief is causing the loop.
- **Step 3:** Where is Lise? Ilsa won't say. But the players have options:
  - Follow Ilsa at 17:00 — she goes into the church, through a side door, into the cellar. Behind the false wall: Lise.
  - Ask Velda about the church cellar after 16:00 (if they've earned her trust by being compassionate or helpful in previous loops). She mentions "the old cellar, behind the wall in the storage room."
  - Go to the church and listen near the cellar door between 13:00-16:00. A DC 12 Perception check hears a child humming.
- **Step 4:** Find Lise. She's been there for weeks. She says: "Mama says the dragon comes because I'm hiding. She says if I come out, it stops." This is the crux: the players must decide what to do with this information.

### Loop 5 — Rally
- They know the truth. Now they need to act.
- **Step 1:** Go to Ilsa first thing at dawn. Tell her they know about Lise and the pact. Ask her what she wants to do. She says: "I want to keep my daughter. But I can't ask the village to burn for me."
- **Step 2:** Go to Dalla at the west gate. Tell her about the pact. She already suspects. She'll say: "I watched them walk a child east 20 years ago. I did nothing. This time I do something."
- **Step 3:** Go to the priestess. Show her the church records (she already has them) and the mayor's letters. Velda is shaken. She says: "I taught the children that apprenticing was a blessing. I will not teach that lie again."
- **Step 4:** Go to the blacksmith. Tell him about the tribute. He says: "My master went east. They said he was going to be a smith in the big city. But I inherited his forge because he never came back." He's in.
- **Step 5:** Confront the mayor. Show him the letters. He panics, tries to flee. Dalla stops him. He confesses everything in front of whoever is gathered.
- **Step 6:** Ilsa walks to the square at 17:30. She tells the village the truth. Lise is with her — outside for the first time in 6 weeks. The village stands with her.
- **Step 7:** 18:00. The dragon comes. It circles. It lands. It speaks: "The pact is broken. The tribute is refused." Ilsa steps forward: "We refuse to give you our children. Not this one. Not any of them." The dragon studies her. Studies the village standing behind her. Then: "I will not collect what is not given. But the protection ends." It flies east.
- **The loop breaks.** The next dawn is real.

**What if they miss a step?** This route is flexible. If they don't find the mayor's letters, they can still get the truth from Ilsa by confronting her with Snik's observation + Dalla's testimony. If they don't earn Velda's trust, they can find the cellar by following Ilsa. The key milestones are: (1) learn about the pact, (2) find Lise, (3) get Ilsa to face the village, (4) get the village to stand with her.

## Route B: Combat (Kill the dragon with prep from previous loops)

**Core idea:** Use the loops as prep time. Learn the dragon's patterns. Forge weapons. Set up allies. Kill it.

### Loop 1 — Die
- Same as Route A. They die. They learn the dragon exists.

### Loop 2 — Observe
- They see the dragon attack again. This time they're watching, not reacting.
- **Step 1:** Position at the east gate or north hill by 17:30. Watch it come from the east. Note the approach angle.
- **Step 2:** If anyone survives past 18:05, they hear the dragon speak: "The pact is broken. The tribute is withheld. I collect." They now know it's intelligent and contract-bound.
- **Step 3:** If they die, note what killed them. Breath weapon? Claws? Tail sweep? This knowledge carries over.

### Loop 3 — Scout
- Now they're testing and gathering intel.
- **Step 1:** Go east at dawn. Find the ridge (2 hours walk). The dragon is asleep in the cave. Don't enter — they can smell sulphur, see claw marks, see it from a distance. They learn: it sleeps during the day. It's active at sunset.
- **Step 2:** Go to the mill at midday. They see the dragon drink at 12:00 and 12:15. It flies low — 30 feet off the ground. They can see the scar under its left jaw. This is the attack window.
- **Step 3:** Find the watchtower on the north hill. The ranger's journal gives them: flight patterns, weak spot (gap under left jaw from old spear wound), and the fact that it flies low over the mill at midday.
- **Step 4:** Observe the dragon circling at 14:30 from the watchtower. Note its size, Spellcasting (if visible), and legendary action patterns (they won't see these yet — they need to fight it once).

### Loop 4 — Prep
- They have the knowledge. Now they need the tools and allies.
- **Step 1 (dawn):** Go to Aldric at the forge. Tell him: "Can you make arrows that pierce scales? We have a design." Give him the design from the ranger's journal (he needs to see the weak spot description — a narrow gap, heavy bolt, fragmentation tip). He says: "Not in one day." This is fine. He'll have them ready on the NEXT loop.
- **Step 2 (morning):** Earn Dalla's trust. She's at the west gate all day. Tell her about the dragon and that it's coming. On loop 3, she might not believe you. On loop 4, say: "Last time, it came from the east at 18:00. It breathes fire. It said 'the pact is broken.' You've been a guard here for 20 years. You know what that means." She believes you. She'll organize the guards.
- **Step 3 (morning):** Earn Velda's trust. Tell her the village is in danger. She's skeptical but not dismissive. If you tell her about the pact and the children, she's shaken. She'll pray for protection if you ask.
- **Step 4 (morning):** Buy supplies from Snik. Acid vials (2d6 damage on hit), a spyglass (to watch the ridge), and the fear ward charm (advantage on one fear save — useful against dragon's Frightful Presence).
- **Step 5 (midday):** Go to the mill. Watch the dragon drink. Confirm the weak spot. Plan positions for the next loop.
- **Step 6 (afternoon):** Tell Dalla the plan. "We're going to fight it at midday tomorrow, at the mill. I need your guards on the north road to prevent it from fleeing to the ridge." Dalla: "You're mad. But I've watched a child walk east and done nothing. If you're going to fight, I'll make sure the road is covered."
- **Step 7 (sunset):** The dragon attacks. The party dies. But they know exactly what they need for the next loop.

### Loop 5 — Execute
- This is the fight. They have:
  - Aldric's dragon-piercing arrows (given the design at dawn on loop 4, ready at midday on loop 5)
  - Dalla coordinating guards on the north road
  - Velda praying for +1 AC
  - Snik's acid vials and fear ward
  - Knowledge of the weak spot, the dragon's schedule, and its breath weapon pattern
  - Positions at the mill by 11:30
- **Step 1:** Set up at the mill. Best archer on the mill roof (30 ft vantage, partial cover). Melee fighters in the treeline. Healer inside the mill (full cover from breath weapon). Supporters with acid vials on the riverbank.
- **Step 2:** 12:00 — the dragon descends to drink. It notices the party. Surprise round if they passed Stealth DC 15.
- **Step 3:** Combat. Use 06-Combat-Encounter.md for tactics. Call out the weak spot. Focus fire on the gap under its left jaw.
- **Step 4:** If the dragon drops below 25% HP, it tries to flee. Dalla's guards on the north road prevent it from reaching the ridge. It's cornered. If they finish it, it dies.
- **Step 5:** The dragon is dead. The loop resets one more time (this is the kill condition — the threat is gone). The next dawn is real.

**What if the fight goes badly?** They die. They loop. They now know the dragon's full combat stats, its spell list, and how it fights. Loop 6 is easier because they've seen everything it can do. The loop is the prep. Let them use it.

## Route C: Renegotiation (Talk to the dragon, offer new terms)

**Core idea:** Learn the pact's terms, approach the dragon peacefully, offer something other than a child.

### Loop 1 — Die
- Same start. Die to the dragon.

### Loop 2 — Observe and learn
- Position to hear the dragon speak at 18:05: "The pact is broken. The tribute is withheld. I collect."
- They now know: it's intelligent, it speaks, it's enforcing a contract. This is the key insight for this route.

### Loop 3 — Investigate the pact
- **Step 1:** Watch Ilsa at 13:00 at the east gate. Notice she stares east.
- **Step 2:** Talk to Snik. He mentions the eastern hills and sulphur.
- **Step 3:** Go to the mayor's open audience at 10:00. Ask about the east. He deflects. Ask about "the pact" or "the arrangement" — he panics slightly and ends the audience.
- **Step 4:** Break into the mayor's office (14:00-15:00, DC 15 lockpick). Find the letters. Learn: every 20 years, a child is sent east as "tribute." The village calls it "apprenticeship." Ilsa refused. The payment is overdue.
- **Step 5:** Find the painted-over mural in the town hall. Scrape or magic-reveal it. Founders shaking hands with a dragon. Children walking toward it. "THE PACT ENDURES."

### Loop 4 — Approach the dragon
- **Step 1 (dawn):** Go east. 2 hours to the ridge. The dragon is in its cave, awake but resting.
- **Step 2:** Approach without weapons drawn. The dragon addresses them: "You are not the tribute. You are not the village elder. Why are you here?"
- **Step 3:** Explain that they know about the pact. Ask what terms would be acceptable instead of a child. The dragon is intrigued — nobody has asked to renegotiate in 200 years. It says: "The form of payment has been revisited before. The original pact was for gold. The village offered children because it was cheaper. I accepted because I was... hungry."
- **Step 4:** The dragon will listen to offers but won't accept just anything. It values:
  - **Service over substance** — a skilled artisan working for it is worth more than gold.
  - **Challenge** — it's been bored for 200 years. A recurring challenge appeals to it.
  - **Equivalent exchange** — something of genuine value, not a token gesture.
- **Step 5:** The dragon demands they return before sunset with an offer. If they don't, it attacks at 18:00 as normal.

### Loop 5 — Negotiate
- They've talked to the dragon. They know what it wants. Now they need to bring an offer.
- **Possible offers:**
  - **A blacksmith's service:** Aldric agrees (or can be convinced) to forge for the dragon periodically. A dragon with a personal smith is a status symbol. The dragon accepts this. Aldric goes east for one month per year.
  - **Gold:** The village treasury + party gold. The dragon accepts 5,000g as equivalent to a lifetime of a child's labor. This requires convincing the mayor to open the treasury (possible if he's confronted with the truth).
  - **A champion's challenge:** "Every 20 years, a champion of Ashen fights you. If they win, the pact is amended. If you win, you take the tribute." The dragon finds this entertaining. It accepts.
  - **The players themselves:** "We will come when you call. We will do services for you. In exchange, no more children." The dragon accepts if the players are genuinely powerful and useful.
  - **Something creative:** The players come up with their own offer. The DM should lean toward accepting creative offers that feel equivalent.
- **The negotiation:** At the ridge. The dragon and the party hammer out terms. The dragon is formal — it speaks like a lawyer reading a contract. It wants specifics. It won't accept vague promises. The terms are spoken aloud and the pact updates (the seal in the well glows, or the mural in the town hall shifts to reflect the new terms).
- **The loop breaks.** The next dawn is real.

**What if the party approaches the dragon with weapons drawn?** It attacks. It's not stupid. If they want to negotiate, they must come in peace. If they try to negotiate mid-combat, it only listens if they have the upper hand (below 25% HP) — and then it's more of a surrender than a negotiation.

**What if the party can't agree on terms?** The dragon gives them until sunset. If no deal is reached, it attacks at 18:00 and the loop resets. They can try again next loop with a better offer.

## What If Players Mix Routes?

They will. That's fine. The most likely mixed route:

- **Loop 1-2:** Die and observe (Route B start)
- **Loop 3:** Find the ranger's journal and learn about Ilsa (mix of Routes A and B)
- **Loop 4:** Approach the dragon, learn it can be negotiated with (Route C)
- **Loop 5:** Offer to renegotiate, but wanting backup — they also prep the blacksmith and guards in case talks fail (Routes B and C combined)

This is the best case. They're prepared for multiple outcomes. Let them hedge their bets. If the negotiation fails, they can still fight. If the fight goes badly, they can still try diplomacy. The loop is forgiving. That's the point.

## The Minimal Path (Fastest Possible Resolution)

If the players are extremely efficient, they can resolve this in 3 loops. This is rare but possible:

### Loop 1 — Die
### Loop 2 — Observe. Learn the dragon comes from the east at sunset. Hear it speak. Know it's contract-bound.
### Loop 3 — Go east at dawn. Talk to the dragon. Make an offer. Resolve.

This requires the players to be bold (approaching a dragon directly with no combat prep) and creative (having a good offer ready). It's possible but unlikely for a first-time group. Most groups will want the security of 5 loops.

---

## GM Notes on Running the Routes

- **Don't force a route.** If they want to fight, let them fight. If they want to talk, let them talk. If they want to do both, let them prep for both.
- **Information is the reward.** Every loop should give them 2-3 new pieces of information. If a loop feels like it gave nothing, you're blocking them. Make the next NPC more talkative. Make the next clue more obvious.
- **The loop is the mechanic.** If they die on loop 2, that's fine. They learned something. If they die on loop 5 during the big fight, they come back with full knowledge of the dragon's combat pattern. That's valuable. Never punish failure — failure is information.
- **Ilsa is the emotional center.** Even if they take Route B (combat), they should discover Ilsa and Lise. Even if they take Route C (negotiation), the dragon should mention that the village has been sending children for 200 years. The emotional truth is the same regardless of the mechanical route.
