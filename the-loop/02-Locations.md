# The Loop — Locations

> Detailed in `01-Timeline.md` per hour. This document covers what's at each location, what's hidden, and what changes if the party interacts.

## The Village of Ashen

Small river town, maybe 200 people. One main road running east-west. A square in the center. The river runs north of town behind the old mill. Hills to the east. Farmland to the west and south.

---

## Town Square

The heart. Flower stall (Ilsa), market stalls, the well. Where the players wake up (if they slept at the inn) or walk into first.

**Every loop:**
- Pia drops apples at 06:00
- Ilsa at her stall until 09:00, then church, then east gate at 13:00, then graveyard at 16:00
- Market stalls from 07:00-15:00

**Hidden:**
- Ilsa's stall has a child's drawing tucked behind the flower buckets — a dragon drawn in crayon. She doesn't notice it. It reappears every loop.
- The well is 40 feet deep. At the bottom: old charred bones. Remains of a fire 200 years ago. If the players investigate, they find a melted metal seal with the town's old crest and draconic script: "PAID IN FULL." This is the original pact marker.

---

## The Copper Mug (Inn)

Run by Bronn. Where the players sleep (if they stay in town). Warm, smoky, cheap.

**Every loop:**
- Bronn wipes the bar at 06:00, serves breakfast at 07:00, gossips all day

**Hidden:**
- Behind the bar, under a loose floorboard: a journal from the previous innkeeper, 20 years ago. The last entry: "They took little Fen today. Carsten said he was going to be a carpenter in the east. The east has no carpenters. I'm pouring drinks and pretending." This is the first written evidence that the "apprenticeships" are not what they seem.

---

## The Forge (Blacksmith)

Run by Aldric. South side of town. Only source of serious metalwork.

**Every loop:**
- Aldric starts the forge at 07:00, opens fully at 08:00, goes to the well at 12:00-12:20

**Hidden:**
- Aldric's master's journal, in a locked chest (DC 15 to pick). It describes forging "piercing arrows for the eastern tribute" — weapons meant for the dragon, not against it. The village used to arm the tribute children, sending them east with weapons. The children never came back. The weapons did — returned rusted and bloodied, sold for scrap.

---

## The Town Hall

Mayor Carsten's domain. Open audience 10:00-11:00, closed council 14:00-15:00.

**Every loop:**
- Mayor walks to the hall at 08:00
- Open audience 10:00-11:00
- Locked during closed council 14:00-15:00

**Hidden:**
- The painted-over mural on the north wall (see Timeline 10:00). If scraped or revealed: shows founders shaking hands with a dragon, children walking toward it. Text: "THE PACT ENDURES."
- Mayor's office, locked (DC 20 to pick during closed council, DC 15 when empty). Inside: letters from "the eastern protectorate" demanding tribute. A ledger with names — one per generation — of children sent east. The last entry is 20 years ago. The next line is blank, waiting for Ilsa's daughter's name. The name "Ilsa" is written in the margin with a question mark, then crossed out, then written again.

---

## The Church

Run by Priestess Velda. Central, stone, old. Bell tower rings at 08:00 and 17:00.

**Every loop:**
- Velda sweeps steps at 06:00
- Children's lessons 11:00-15:00
- Bell at 17:00 (sunset warning)

**Hidden:**
- Side room with church records (accessible if Velda trusts them after 16:00). Records mention "the arrangement" in archaic language every 20 years. Last entry: 20 years ago. Next entry: blank.
- **The cellar.** Behind a false wall at the back of the storage cellar. If traced by sound (Ilsa's daughter hums between 13:00-16:00 — only audible if the church is empty and the players are near the cellar door) or if Ilsa is followed at 17:00. Inside: Ilsa's daughter, Lise, age 8. Scared. Drawing dragons. Has been here for weeks. Details in `05-The-Secret.md`.

---

## The Mill

Old water mill on the river north of town. No longer grinds grain — the wheel still turns but nobody uses it. 15 minutes walk from town square.

**Every loop:**
- Abandoned and quiet all day
- Dragon drinks here at 12:00 and 12:15 — only visible if players go to the mill area

**Hidden:**
- The miller's old quarters upstairs have a journal describing "the great beast that drinks from the river at noon" — observations from 20 years ago. The miller is gone. "Apprenticed."
- From the upstairs window, clear line of sight to the dragon's underbelly when it drinks. The scar under its left jaw is visible. This is where players learn the weak spot.

---

## The East Gate and Road

Guarded by Pim (nervous, young) during the day. The road leads to the eastern ridge — 2 hours walk.

**Every loop:**
- Holt at gate 06:00, Pim takes over 07:00
- Patrol leaves 13:00, returns 16:00
- Ilsa stands here staring east 13:00-13:30

**Hidden:**
- Old claw marks above the gate archway. Holt says "old war damage." They're not.
- 1 hour down the east road: an abandoned watchtower. Inside: a ranger's journal from 20 years ago describing the dragon's flight patterns, its weak spot (gap under left jaw from an old spear wound), and that it flies low over the mill at midday to drink. The ranger's final entry: "It came for the tribute. I tried to stop it. If you're reading this, do better." His bones are outside the tower, picked clean.
- 2 hours down the road: the dragon's ridge. A large rocky outcrop with a cave. Smell of sulphur. Scorched earth. The dragon is here from sunset to dawn. During the day (after 12:00 drinking) it circles the area. If approached, it perceives intruders. It will not attack unless provoked during the day, but it watches.

---

## The Graveyard

Behind the church. Small. Maybe 50 graves.

**Every loop:**
- Ilsa visits 16:00-16:15, stands at an unmarked grave with a carved flower

**Hidden:**
- Ilsa's husband's grave. He died 3 years ago. Cause of death listed in church records: "fever." But the grave is separate from the other fever victims — placed at the edge, near the east wall, facing the ridge. When Ilsa is here, she whispers the same line every loop: "Keep her safe. Keep her hidden."
- Three other graves, unmarked, at the far east corner. All from different years, all children's sizes. No names. These are the previous tributes that were recovered — or returned.
