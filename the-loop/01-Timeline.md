# The Loop — Timeline

This is the **default day**. Every loop starts here. Unless the party changes something, every event below happens at the same time, in the same way.

The loop resets at **sunset (18:00)** when the dragon arrives, or when the entire party dies — whichever comes first.

## Dawn — 06:00

### What the players experience on waking

They wake up wherever they went to sleep (inn, camp outside town, wherever — and every loop they wake up in the same place). The first thing they hear: birds. Then a cart wheel on cobblestone. Then:

> A child drops a basket of apples in the square. They scatter across the cobblestones, rolling into the gutter. The child — a girl, maybe eight, red hair, patched dress — scrambles after them, laughing.

**The apple child is Pia.** She's Ilsa's neighbor's daughter. She is NOT Ilsa's daughter. But she's the first sign of normalcy and the first thing that changes if the party interacts with the morning differently.

### Who is where at dawn

| Who | Where | What they're doing |
|-----|-------|--------------------|
| Pia (apple child) | Town square | Dropping apples, laughing |
| Ilsa | Flower stall in the square, east side | Arranging flowers, humming |
| Bronn (innkeeper) | The Copper Mug inn | Wiping down the bar, yawning |
| Aldric (blacksmith) | Forge, south side of town | Opening shutters, starting the fire |
| Watchman Holt | East gate | Leaning on his spear, watching the road |
| Priestess Velda | Church steps | Sweeping the steps |
| Mayor Carsten | Mayor's residence | Reading letters at his desk (not visible to players unless they break in) |

### What the players can observe (if they pay attention)

- Ilsa hums the same tune every loop. It's a lullaby. The same one her daughter hums in the cellar.
- The flower stall has a small drawing tucked behind the buckets — a child's drawing of a dragon, similar to the ones in the cellar. Ilsa doesn't notice it's there. If the players point it out, she says "Children draw the strangest things" and puts it away. Every loop, it reappears.
- The east gate has old claw marks on the stone above the archway. Holt will say it's "old damage from the war" if asked.

## Early Morning — 07:00

### What changes

The market carts arrive. Blacksmith's forge starts smoking. The smell of bread from the baker.

### Key events

- **Market setup.** Three market stalls: produce (run by a halfling named Tess), cloth (run by a tired human named Maren), and trinkets (run by a goblin named Snik, who is technically not supposed to be in town but nobody cares). Snik knows things — he travels. If the players talk to him, he mentions "a big shape in the eastern hills" and "the smell of sulphur on the wind last night." He says this every loop, but only if asked about the east.
- **Guard shift change.** The night watch goes home. Two new guards take the east and west gates. The east guard is named Pim, a young man who is nervous and new. The west guard is Dalla, a dwarf veteran who has seen things. Dalla is one of the few villagers who suspects the truth — she watched a child walk east 20 years ago and never come back. She won't volunteer this. But if the players earn her trust (by being competent, or by saving someone in a previous loop), she'll say: "Every twenty years, a child leaves this town. They call it apprenticing. I call it something else."

---

## Morning — 08:00

### Key events

- **Blacksmith Aldric opens the forge fully.** He's a big, quiet man. If the players ask about weapons, he says he makes "plows and hinges, not swords." But he has the skill — he's the only one in town who could forge dragon-piercing ammunition. On loop 1, there's no reason to ask. On loop 4+, if the players bring him a design and say "can you make this by midday," he'll say "not in one day." But on the *next* loop, they can give him the design at dawn, and he'll have it ready by midday. The loop is the prep.
- **Mayor Carsten walks from his residence to the town hall.** He's a tall, thin man who smiles too much. He waves at people. He ignores Ilsa. This is noticeable if the players are watching — he greets everyone except the flower woman. Every loop.
- **Priestess Velda rings the church bell.** Three rings. The sound carries across the whole village. It marks the start of the day for most residents. Velda is kind but firm. She knows about the deal in a vague way — the church records mention "the arrangement" but not the specifics. If the players ask to see the records, she'll say they'd need the mayor's permission. If they push, she'll let them into the church after hours (see 16:00).

---

## Late Morning — 09:00

### Key events

- **Travelers arrive at the east gate.** Two merchants in a cart, heading west through town. They're chatty. They'll mention "strange weather to the east" and "a smell like rotten eggs on the ridge." This is the dragon's territory. If the players ask them to detour or stay, they refuse — they have goods to deliver. They leave town by the west gate by 10:00. If the players try to warn them about the dragon, they laugh it off: "A dragon? In this economy?"
- **Ilsa moves from the flower stall to the church.** She goes inside and prays. If followed, the players can hear her praying. It sounds normal — blessings for the harvest, for the village. But every loop, she ends with the same whispered line: "Keep her safe. Keep her hidden." She doesn't realize she says it out loud.

---

## Mid-Morning — 10:00

### Key events

- **Mayor Carsten holds open audience in the town hall.** Anyone can come with complaints or requests. This is the easiest way to talk to him. He's dismissive, charming, and guarded. If the players ask about the east, he says "fine hunting grounds." If they ask about the painted-over mural in the hall, he says "old decoration, irrelevant." If they push harder, he ends the audience early. If they come back on a later loop with the letters from his office, he panics. The audience runs until 11:00.
- **The painted-over mural is on the north wall of the town hall.** It depicts the founding of Ashen. In the corner, partially visible through the peeling paint, is a dragon. A figure is shaking hands with it. The paint is thick but if someone scrapes it or uses magic, the full image appears: the village founders, the dragon, and a line of children walking toward it. Below, in old script: "THE PACT ENDURES."

---

## Approaching Midday — 11:00

### Key events

- **Children are at lessons with the priestess in the church.** Velda teaches them letters and numbers. If the players observe, they'll notice there are no children above age 7 or 8 in the lesson. The older children are "apprenticed" — the word hangs in the air. If they ask Velda about this, she says: "It's the way of things here. Children go to learn trades in the east." She says it warmly, like it's a good thing. Because she genuinely believes it is. She doesn't know the truth. Nobody in the village does — they all think it's just tradition.
- **The market is at its busiest.** Tess, Maren, and Snik are all active. Snik has the best information — he's traveled the eastern road within the last week. He'll describe "bones near the ridge" and "a cave that smells like fire" if asked about the east. He'll also buy information — if the players tell him something from a future loop, he reacts normally, then pauses and says "you talk like you've been here before." He's the only NPC who senses the loop. He doesn't know why. It unnerves him.

---

## Midday — 12:00

### Key events

- **The dragon drinks at the mill.** This is the crucial window. The dragon flies low over the river behind the old mill, twice per loop, at exactly 12:00 and 12:15. It drinks for about 30 seconds each time. From the village, you can't see this — it's behind the treeline. But if the players go to the mill area (north of town, 15 minutes walk), they can witness it. On loop 1, they have no reason to be there. On loop 3+, this is where they learn the dragon's flight pattern, weak spot visibility (the scar under its left jaw is visible when it dips to drink), and that it's real and enormous.
- **Blacksmith Aldric goes to the well for water.** He's at the well from 12:00-12:20. This is when he's most talkative — he's taking a break. If the players talk to him here, he mentions that his old master used to make "things that could pierce anything — scales, armor, stone." He doesn't say dragon. He doesn't have to. He also mentions his master "left on apprenticeship, like everyone does" and never came back. He got the forge because the previous smith disappeared. He says this casually. It's not casual.

---

## Early Afternoon — 13:00

### Key events

- **Ilsa walks to the east gate and stands there, staring east.** She does this every loop between 13:00 and 13:30. She doesn't move. She doesn't speak. She just stares at the eastern ridge. If approached, she startles and says "the view is nice this time of day." It's not. The eastern road is flat and grey. She's watching for the dragon. She doesn't know this consciously.
- **Guard patrol leaves through the east gate.** Two guards — Pim and an older guard named Reese — walk the eastern road to the ridge and back. They leave at 13:00 and return at 16:00. On loop 1, they come back shaken. They won't say why. On loop 3+, if the players go with them or question them separately, Reese admits: "Something big moved up there. We didn't stay to find out what." Pim is terrified and won't go past the first mile marker. This is useful information — the dragon is active on the ridge during this window.

---

## Afternoon — 14:00

### Key events

- **Mayor Carsten holds closed council in his office.** The door is locked. He meets with two council members (Regina, a severe woman who runs the general store, and old Holt, the retired watchman). They discuss "the arrangement." If the players break in or eavesdrop (DC 15 Perception to hear through the door, DC 20 to pick the lock), they hear fragments: "she refused," "the deadline was last month," "if we give the girl ourselves, it ends." If confronted, Carsten denies everything: "council business is council business."
- **The dragon circles overhead on clear days.** Around 14:30, if it's not overcast, a shadow passes over the town. Most NPCs don't look up. The dragon is circling high — not attacking, just watching. It does this every loop. On overcast loops, the shadow is absent but a low distant roar can be heard. If the players are on the north hill (watchtower), they can see it clearly through the broken ceiling.

---

## Mid-Afternoon — 15:00

### Key events

- **Children finish lessons.** They file out of the church, ages 5-7, then scatter to their homes. Velda stays behind to clean. If the players talk to Velda now, she's more relaxed. She mentions "the old cellar" if asked about storage for supplies — "beneath the church, behind the old wall." She doesn't know Ilsa's daughter is there. But she knows the cellar exists.
- **The market winds down.** Tess packs up first (by 15:30). Maren finishes folding cloth by 16:00. Snik lingers until 16:30, selling last trinkets at a discount. Snik is the last person the players can buy from — he sells vials of acid, a spyglass, and a "lucky charm" that's actually a minor ward against fear (gives advantage on one fear save). None of these persist across loops.

---

## Late Afternoon — 16:00

### Key events

- **Ilsa visits the graveyard.** She walks from the east gate to the small graveyard behind the church. She stands at one grave for 15 minutes. The grave is unmarked except for a carved flower. If the players investigate, they find out it's the grave of her husband, who died three years ago. The carved flower matches the flowers she sells. She doesn't cry. She just stands there. If the players talk to her, she's calm: "He wanted her to go. Said it was an honor. Said every family gives a child to the east. I couldn't." If they ask who "her" is, she clams up. But she's said more than she meant to.
- **Guard patrol returns.** Reese and Pim come back through the east gate. Reese looks troubled. Pim looks terrified. This is the party's last chance to question them before sunset.
- **Priestess Velda can be convinced to open the church records.** If the players have earned her trust (saved someone, shown genuine concern, or mentioned specific knowledge that proves they're not random troublemakers), she'll let them into the side room after 16:00. The records mention "the arrangement" in archaic language: every 20 years, "a tribute to the eastern guardian" is sent. The last entry was 20 years ago. The next entry is blank — waiting for a name.

---

## Evening — 17:00

### Key events

- **The sunset bells ring.** Velda rings the church bell once. It's the signal for the village to close up. Market stalls are gone. Doors are shut. The guards at both gates tighten up. The village feels smaller.
- **If the players warned people about the dragon, the warning fails.** On loop 2, they try to warn. Nobody believes them. On loop 3+, if they have proof (the mayor's letters, Snik's testimony, the watchtower journal), some NPCs listen. But mobilizing the village takes longer than they have. They have one hour. The guards will listen to Dalla's command if the players have her on their side. The blacksmith will forge if he's had prep time (from a previous loop). The priestess will pray for protection (it gives +1 AC to everyone in the church). But the village as a whole cannot be convinced to evacuate in one hour.
- **Ilsa disappears.** Where she goes, nobody knows. She leaves her stall, walks toward the church, and doesn't come out. If followed, she goes into the church, through a side door, and down into the cellar — where her daughter is hiding. The players can follow her here. If they do, they find the hidden room. They find the girl. This triggers the reveal (see 05-The-Secret.md).

---

## Sunset — 18:00

### The dragon arrives

The dragon comes from the east, flying low over the ridge. It takes approximately 2 minutes from first sight to reaching the village. The players see it before the NPCs do — if they're on the north hill or at the east gate, they get a 5-minute warning.

**What the dragon does:**

1. **First pass (18:00):** Flyover, breathing fire across the market square. This destroys stalls, sets buildings alight, and kills anyone in the open. NPCs who are inside survive the first pass.
2. **Second pass (18:03):** Targets the church and the mayor's residence. This is personal — the dragon knows where the deal was made.
3. **Landing (18:05):** The dragon lands in the square and speaks one line in Draconic, then Common: "The pact is broken. The tribute is withheld. I collect." Then it begins destroying methodically — not hunting, not raging. Collecting. It's enforcing a contract.
4. **Aftermath (18:08+):** The village is rubble. Anyone who fights dies unless they're extremely powerful or extremely prepared. The loop resets at the moment of total destruction or party death.

**What the players can do at sunset:**

- **Fight it unprepared (loop 1-2):** They die. Quickly. The dragon is adult-level and ruthless. This is fine. They loop.
- **Run (any loop):** They can flee. The dragon doesn't chase fleeing individuals — it's focused on the village. But the loop still resets at 18:00 because the village is destroyed.
- **Fight it prepared (loop 5+):** See 06-Combat-Encounter.md.
- **Confront Ilsa before sunset:** If they've found Ilsa's daughter and brought her to the square, the dragon lands, sees her, and waits. This starts the diplomatic or renegotiation path (see 07-Endings.md).

## Notes for Running the Timeline

- **The players will NOT see everything in one loop.** That's the point. Each loop they learn 2-3 new things. By loop 5, they've assembled the picture.
- **Describe the same details each loop.** The apples rolling. Ilsa humming. The forge smoke. The bell. These become comforting, then ominous, then meaningful.
- **Let them skip ahead.** If they say "we go straight to the mayor's office at dawn," let them. The timeline is their reference, not a leash. Things still happen in the background.
- **Track what they've changed.** After each loop, note what they did differently. NPCs who were told about the dragon will remember nothing. But the players remember everything.
