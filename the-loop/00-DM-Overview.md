# The Loop — DM Overview

## Pitch

The party wakes up in the village of Ashen at dawn. Birds singing. Market stalls opening. A kid drops a basket of apples in the street. By sunset, a dragon destroys the village. Everyone dies. Then they wake up at dawn again. Same birds. Same kid. Same apples. They're stuck in a time loop, and they have as many tries as they need to figure out how to save Ashen — or whether it can be saved at all.

## Genre

Groundhog Day meets D&D

## Length

3-4 hours (5-7 loops)

## Players

4, levels 3-5

## Tone

Start warm and cozy. The village should feel real and worth saving. Let the players like the NPCs before they die. The horror is in the repetition — the same kind market woman, the same laughing child, the same sunset. The warmth turns to dread turns to determination.

## Core Rules

- **Each loop is one in-game day (dawn to sunset).** Track time roughly: morning, midday, afternoon, evening, sunset.
- **When they die or the dragon arrives, reset.** Players keep all knowledge. HP, spell slots, and items reset to their starting state.
- **NPCs repeat their routines every loop** unless the party changes something. The blacksmith always goes to the well at noon. The mayor always holds council at 2pm. The dragon always comes from the east at sunset.
- **The loop is the puzzle.** There is no single investigation scene. The entire day is the investigation. Every loop they learn more.
- **The players cannot outrun the loop.** If they leave town, they still reset at sunset. The village must be saved.

## The Secret

Ashen traded its firstborn children to a dragon 200 years ago for protection during a war. The war ended. The deal didn't. The dragon returns every generation to collect, and the village sends a child away and calls it "apprenticeship." This generation, a mother (Ilsa) refused. She hid her daughter. The dragon came anyway. The loop is her grief, turned into a curse. She doesn't know she's doing it.

## Three Valid Endings

1. **Diplomatic:** Help Ilsa and her daughter break the curse. The dragon doesn't come. The day doesn't reset.
2. **Combat:** Kill the dragon with prep earned across loops. The threat is gone. The day doesn't reset.
3. **Renegotiation:** Confront Ilsa, learn the truth, then talk to the dragon. It's intelligent — it made a contract. It might accept different terms.
