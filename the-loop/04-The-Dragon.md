# The Loop — The Dragon

> The dragon is the timer, the threat, and a negotiating partner — depending on how the players approach it.

---

## Overview

An adult dragon bound by a 200-year-old magical contract. It's not evil in the traditional sense — it made a deal and expects payment. It's been coming to Ashen for generations. Every time, the village gives it a child. This time, the child is hidden. The dragon came anyway.

---

## Stats

Use an Adult Red Dragon stat block (or equivalent for your system of choice) with the following modifications:

- **HP:** As standard, but it has regeneration 10 while in its lair (the eastern ridge)
- **Weak spot:** The gap in the scales under its left jaw. Attacks targeting this specific area have advantage if the attacker knows about it (from the ranger's journal at the watchtower). A hit here deals an extra 2d10 piercing damage and the dragon reels — it can't use its legendary actions on its next turn.
- **Behavior:** The dragon is intelligent and speaks Common and Draconic. It will talk if approached with the intent to negotiate. It will not parley during combat.

## Daily Schedule

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Dawn - 11:00 | At the ridge, sleeping |
| 12:00 & 12:15 | Drinks at the mill (flies low over the river) |
| 14:30 | Circles Ashen (visible from watchtower on clear days) |
| 18:00 | Attacks from the east |

## What Players Learn Per Loop

| Loop | What's discoverable | How |
|------|-------------------|-----|
| 1 | The dragon exists. It's enormous. It's real. | It destroys the village at 18:00. |
| 2 | It comes from the east at sunset. Reliable pattern. | Can position to observe. |
| 3 | It drinks at the mill at midday. It sleeps at the ridge. It circles at 14:30. The watchtower has a dead ranger's journal describing its weak spot. | Go east, visit the mill, climb the watchtower. |
| 4 | It speaks. It's enforcing a contract. It said "the pact is broken, the tribute is withheld." It's not mindless. | Survive long enough to hear it speak (18:05). Or get close enough to the ridge to be addressed. |
| 5+ | It can be negotiated with. It made a deal. It might accept different terms. | Approach it at the ridge (daytime, not hostile). Or bring Lise to the square before sunset. |

---

## What the Dragon Knows

- The exact terms of the 200-year-old pact.
- That Ilsa is withholding the tribute.
- That the village continues to send children. It considers this acceptable payment.
- It has no malice toward the village. It considers this a business arrangement. The pact doesn't require it to feel good about this.

---

## Speech Patterns

The dragon speaks slowly, formally, like a lawyer reading a contract it's read a thousand times.

**First words upon landing (18:05):**
> "The pact is broken. The tribute is withheld. I collect."

**If approached at the ridge during the day:**
> "You are not the tribute. You are not the village elder. Why are you here?"

**If someone tries to renegotiate:**
> "The pact is clear. Firstborn. Every generation. This is not negotiable. ...But the form of payment has been... revisited before."

**If asked about the children it has taken:**
> "They served. As was agreed." (Long pause.) "The first ones screamed. The later ones came willingly. That was worse."

**If the players offer to fight it:**
> "You may try. The last one who tried left a mark. Under my jaw. Do you see it?" (It tilts its head, showing the scar.) "I remember him. He was brave."

---

## Dragon Flight Patterns (Detailed)

For players who observe carefully across loops:

- **06:00-11:59:** At the ridge, in the cave. Asleep or resting. Can be approached (DC 18 Stealth to get close without waking; it's a light sleeper). If woken, it will address the party with curiosity, not hostility — "You came to me. Unusual."
- **12:00-12:15:** Drinks at the mill. Flies low (30 ft off the ground). This is the best window for a ranged attack if they've set up at the mill.
- **14:30:** Circles the town high above (200 ft). Visible from the watchtower. Not attackable at this range but observable — they can confirm its size, flight pattern, and that it's watching the village.
- **18:00:** Attacks from the east at full speed. No stealth. No surprise. It wants the village to see it coming.

## Combat Difficulty by Loop

| Loop | Can they fight it? | Why/why not |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| 1-2 | No | They don't know its patterns, weak spot, or that it exists before 18:00. They die. |
| 3 | Unlikely | They know the schedule and weak spot but have no prep. A straight fight is still likely a TPK. |
| 4-5 | Possible | They have the ranger's journal, they know the weak spot, and they can prep the blacksmith in the previous loop. With coordinated village help, it's a hard fight but winnable. |
| 5+ | Winnable | Full knowledge. Prep from previous loops. Dragon-piercing arrows. Positioned allies. They've earned this. |
