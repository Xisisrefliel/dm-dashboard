# The Loop — NPCs

> Every NPC has a routine, a secret, and a point of view on "the arrangement." Most don't know the full truth. They know their piece.

---

## Ilsa — The Mother (Flower Seller)

**Who she is:** Mid-40s, weathered but warm. Sells flowers from a stall in the square. Has a gentle voice and a lullaby she hums without realizing. The village likes her. The mayor avoids her.

**Routine:** See Timeline. Key moments: stall at dawn, church at 09:00, east gate at 13:00, graveyard at 16:00, disappears into the church cellar at 17:00.

**Secret:** She's hiding her daughter Lise in the church cellar to prevent her from being "apprenticed." She doesn't know about the pact or the curse. She just knows her daughter was supposed to go east and she said no. She's not causing the loop — the pact's curse activated because the tribute was withheld, regardless of who withheld it or why. But she's the first mother in ten generations to refuse.

**What she knows:**
- The village has a tradition of sending firstborns east. She believes it's wrong.
- The dragon is coming for her daughter. She won't say this outright until confronted with the mayor's letters.
- She's had conversations "before" — she feels the loop on some level. *"I've had this conversation before. I've had it so many times."*

**How she reacts across loops:**
- **Loop 1:** Friendly, unremarkable. Just the flower woman.
- **Loop 2-3:** Confused when the players know things. Starts wondering if they're prophets or mad.
- **Loop 4+:** If confronted with the letters, she breaks. Reveals Lise. Says the line about having the conversation before — she doesn't remember the loops, but something in her registers the repetition.
- **If Lise is found without Ilsa's knowledge:** She panics. Becomes protective. Will try to take Lise and run. The players need to earn Ilsa's trust or the encounter goes badly.

---

## Lise — The Daughter (Hidden in Church Cellar)

**Who she is:** 8 years old. Red hair like Pia (which is why the apple scene echoes her). Scared but not broken. Has been in the cellar for weeks. Draws pictures of dragons compulsively.

**What she says:** *"Mama says the dragon comes because I'm hiding. She says if I come out, it stops."* This is the curse mechanized — her belief that her hiding is the cause, amplified by Ilsa's grief into literal time magic.

**What she draws:** Dragons. Over and over. The same dragon. The same scene — a dragon flying over houses, and a small figure standing in front of it. Sometimes the figure is a child. Sometimes it's her mother. The drawings are on every surface in the cellar — walls, floor, scraps of paper.

**What happens if the players find her:**
- She's scared but curious. She'll talk. She'll ask if the dragon is gone.
- She'll say she can hear the day restarting. "The birds always start singing at the same time. I hate that part."
- She's the key to breaking the loop, but only if Ilsa confronts the truth.

## Mayor Carsten — The Keeper of the Deal

**Who he is:** Tall, thin, smiles too much. He's the mayor because his family has always been the mayor. The deal is his inheritance. He maintains it because ending it means admitting what it costs.

**Routine:** Residence to town hall at 08:00. Open audience 10:00-11:00. Closed council 14:00-15:00. At his residence the rest of the time.

**Secret:** He knows the full truth — the pact, the dragon, the children. He's the only villager besides Dalla who knows. He's been managing it for 20 years. Ilsa is the first mother to refuse. He's terrified — not of the dragon, but of the village learning the truth.

**What he knows:**
- The names of every child sent east in the last 200 years.
- That the dragon will come regardless of whether Ilsa gives up Lise — the pact demands tribute, and the tribute is overdue.
- That the letters in his office prove everything. He should have burned them. He kept them because he's a bureaucrat who can't bring himself to destroy records.

**How he reacts:**
- **Casual questions:** Deflects. "Old history. Village business."
- **Pressed with evidence:** Panics. Threatens to have them arrested. Then bribes. Then begs.
- **If the village finds out:** He flees. If cornered, he tells the truth — all of it — in a rush, like he's been waiting 20 years for someone to make him.

## Aldric — The Blacksmith

**Who he is:** Big, quiet, capable. Makes plows and hinges. Could make weapons if asked. Doesn't want to.

**Routine:** Forge from 07:00-12:00, well break 12:00-12:20, back to forge until 17:00.

**Secret:** His predecessor was "apprenticed" 20 years ago. He inherited the forge. He's never said it out loud, but he knows the man didn't leave for a better life.

**What he knows:**
- His old master could make arrows that "pierce anything." He has the knowledge but hasn't used it.
- The eastern road is bad news. Blacksmiths don't go east.
- If the players bring him a design for dragon-piercing ammunition, he CAN make it — but not in one day. He needs the design at dawn to have it by midday. This is where the loop matters: they give him the design on loop 4, he has it ready on loop 5.

## Priestess Velda — The Church

**Who she is:** Kind, practical, mid-50s. Runs the church, teaches the children, rings the bell. The village's moral center.

**Routine:** Sweeps steps at 06:00, bell at 08:00, lessons 11:00-15:00, available after 16:00 if trust is earned.

**Secret:** The church records mention "the arrangement." She doesn't know the full truth — she knows children go east and doesn't ask why. Like everyone else in the village, she thinks it's just tradition. She's been complicit by not asking. This eats at her.

**What she knows:**
- The church records in the side room (accessible after 16:00 with her permission).
- The cellar exists. She doesn't know Lise is there.
- The word "apprenticeship" is church language. She teaches the children to accept it. She's ashamed of this.

---

## Dalla — The Veteran Guard (West Gate)

**Who she is:** Dwarf, old, scarred. Has seen things. She was a guard here 20 years ago during the last "apprenticeship."

**Routine:** West gate all day. Doesn't leave her post.

**Secret:** She watched a child walk east 20 years ago and did nothing. She's suspected the truth for a long time — that "apprenticeships" aren't what the village tells itself. She's never forgiven herself.

**What she knows:**
- The village sends children east. She doesn't call it apprenticeship. She calls it "the toll."
- If the players earn her trust (by being competent or saving someone in a previous loop), she'll tell them about the toll and say: "Every twenty years. Always a child. Always firstborn. I stood here and watched."
- She won't fight the dragon — she's too old. But she'll organize the other guards if the players give her a plan.

---

## Watchman Holt — The Retired Guard

**Who he is:** Old, steady, was a guard for 40 years. Now he does the early shift at the east gate (06:00-07:00). Then he goes home.

**Secret:** He was the one who escorted the last tribute child east, 20 years ago. He walked the child to the ridge. He watched the dragon take them. He came back alone.

**What he knows:**
- The exact location of the dragon's ridge (2 hours east).
- That the child went willingly. That he told them they were going to learn a trade.
- He'll only share this if the players ask directly about "the apprenticeship" and show they already know something is wrong. Otherwise he says nothing.

---

## Pim — The Young Guard (East Gate)

**Who he is:** Early 20s, nervous, new to the job. Replaced the last guard who was "apprenticed."

**Routine:** East gate 07:00-13:00. Goes on east patrol 13:00-16:00.

**Secret:** None. He's genuinely new and scared. He heard something big in the eastern hills during patrol. He won't go past the first mile marker.

**What he knows:** Only that something is wrong in the east. He's too scared to investigate.

---

## Snik — The Goblin Merchant

**Who he is:** A goblin trader who passes through Ashen on a circuit. Sells trinkets, information, and oddments. Technically not supposed to be in a human town but nobody cares.

**Routine:** Market stall 07:00-16:30.

**Secret:** He's the only NPC who senses the loop. If the players tell him something from a future loop, he pauses and says: "You talk like you've been here before." It unnerves him.

**What he knows:**
- Big shape in the eastern hills. Smell of sulphur on the wind last night.
- Bones near the ridge. A cave that smells like fire.
- He's traveled the eastern road. He's seen the dragon's territory. He won't go near it again.
- He sells: vials of acid (5g), a spyglass (25g), a "lucky charm" that gives advantage on one fear save (10g, single use).

## Tess (Produce) and Maren (Cloth) — Market Sellers

Background NPCs. Friendly, chatty, no secrets. Tess will gossip about the mayor ("he's been tense lately"). Maren will mention Ilsa ("poor thing, her husband died, and now she barely speaks to anyone"). Both mention that children "go east for apprenticeships" like it's normal — because to them, it is normal. They don't know the truth.

---

## Regina — Council Member (General Store Owner)

Severe, practical. In the mayor's closed council. She knows about the arrangement and supports it: "One child every twenty years to protect two hundred people. That's math." She won't budge unless the players can offer an alternative that protects the village.

---

## Pia — The Apple Child

8 years old, red hair, patched dress. Drops apples in the square at 06:00 every loop. She's not Ilsa's daughter — she's the neighbor's kid. But she looks enough like Lise that observant players might notice. Pia is the emotional anchor. If the players get attached to the laughing kid who drops apples, the dragon destroying the village hits harder on loop 1.
